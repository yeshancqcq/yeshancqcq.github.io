About Me

I have hybrid educational backgrounds in geosciences and geography (especially in GIS and cartography). I regard them as keys to unlock mysteries of our planet earth. In past years, I have developed my interests in understanding how landforms and climate systems were generated, how they would change in the long-run future, and how the physical environments would interact with human activities. As a geologist with solid knowledge in GIS, I have slightly different approaches to address geological questions. In addition to traditional ways of geological thinking and reasoning which are usually temporal-oriented, I often like to think in a spatial-oriented perspective and to bring in spatial analysis techniques to solve geological puzzles. Currently, I am a PhD student in the Department of Geoscience at University of Wisconsin-Madison focusing on climate change and paleoclimatology. I utilize NLP and ML methods to pull out paleoclimate data from various journals in order to do the meta analysis. My advisors are Dr. Shaun Marcott and Dr. Shanan Peters. My works at Wisconsin are mainly related to EarthCube, GeoDeepDive and Macrostrat projects. Please check out their websites for more information.
